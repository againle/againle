## Upcoming Research: Tame Geometry and Optimization for AI-Generated Data

*Expected Research Period: 2026–2027*

### Motivation

Modern AI systems generate increasingly complex data distributions through large-scale models. However, the underlying mathematical structures governing these patterns remain poorly understood. This project aims to explore whether tools from **tame geometry** and **optimization theory** can provide new perspectives for analyzing the structure, regularity, and complexity of AI-generated data.

### Research Objectives

- Investigate geometric structures underlying high-dimensional representations produced by AI models.
- Study whether AI-generated data distributions exhibit mathematical regularity properties characterizable through tame geometry.
- Analyze the relationship between optimization dynamics and the geometric structure of learned representations.
- Develop theoretical insights connecting optimization, representation learning, and explainable AI.

### Potential Directions

1. **Geometric Analysis of AI Representations**: Study latent spaces generated by neural networks and foundation models.
2. **Optimization Dynamics and Representation Formation**: Connect optimization trajectories with geometric properties of model representations.
3. **Explainability Through Mathematical Structure**: Explore whether geometric properties can provide interpretable explanations of AI-generated outputs.
